About Me / 01

My Journey.

My name is Prastyo Arlan, a Geological Engineering graduate from Institut Teknologi Sumatera with a strong interest in geological exploration, subsurface interpretation, and mining engineering. Throughout my academic journey, I developed a solid foundation in geological mapping, structural geology, geomorphology, hydrogeology, and geotechnical studies through laboratory work, field investigations, and research.

“Turning geological observation into engineering decisions.”

My professional experience began at PT Pertamina Hulu Rokan, where I contributed to well log interpretation, biostratigraphy, electrofacies analysis, sequence stratigraphy, and paleogeographic reconstruction. Working with subsurface geological data strengthened my understanding of basin evolution and showed me how geological observations can be translated into meaningful interpretations that support hydrocarbon exploration.

Alongside my exploration background, I have continued to expand my technical capabilities in geological modeling, GIS, and mining software. I regularly use ArcGIS for spatial analysis and geological mapping, Minescape for resource modeling and mine design, Spry for production scheduling, Global Mapper for terrain analysis, and Microsoft Excel for technical data processing. These tools help me connect geological understanding with engineering and operational planning. Beyond technical skills, I have also developed leadership and communication experience through roles as a laboratory assistant and a member of student organizations. Teaching practical courses, mentoring students, coordinating field activities, and organizing technical events have strengthened my teamwork, responsibility, and problem-solving abilities in collaborative environments. Today, I continue to strengthen my expertise in GIS, mine planning, and geological modeling through tools such as ArcGIS, Minescape, Spry, and Global Mapper. I enjoy learning new technologies and applying geological knowledge to solve practical challenges across both the energy and mining sectors. Turning geological observation into engineering decisions remains at the core of my work, whether that means building a stratigraphic framework, developing a resource block model, or generating a detailed surface in ArcGIS.
